## Template Usage Notes

- **This is a structural guide, not a content source.** All content comes from the user's skill checklist selections mapped through the inventory categories.
- **This template is for the AI Builder variant** — PMs who code, prototype, and build AI products hands-on. For traditional PMs who leverage AI tools for productivity, use `pm-jd-template-traditional.md`.
- **Always differentiate L3 vs L4** through scope of impact, stakeholder level, strategic ownership, account growth responsibility, and experience.
- **L4 must signal:** strategic thinking, client advisory, trust-building, account growth, and team support across R&P — not just technical execution at a higher altitude.
- **Coding requirements are intentional** for this variant — but frame the role as PM-first, not engineer-first. The coding serves product outcomes.
- **Domain section (7) must be customized per engagement** — AI technologies evolve fast, so keep this current.
- **Keep responsibilities action-oriented** — every bullet should start with a verb.
- **Use "Product Manager" as the title** with an AI qualifier (e.g., "Senior Product Manager, Agentic AI").
- **Omit company boilerplate sections** — this template covers PM-specific content only.
- **Do not include skill numbers** in the final JD — these are internal references only.
