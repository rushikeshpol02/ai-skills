## 2. About the Role (3–5 sentences)

Briefly answer:

- What is the product or problem space?
- What kind of team will they work with?
- What does success look like in this role?
- `[L4]` What is the strategic scope and how does this person create value beyond the immediate engagement?

> **Tone:** Outcome-oriented, clear on scope, avoids generic agency language.

## 3. Impact & Scope (2 lines)

> `[L3]` You own product delivery within a single client engagement. You make daily prioritization decisions, run the backlog, and are accountable for sprint-level outcomes.

> `[L4]` You shape product strategy across engagements, advise client stakeholders, and contribute to R&P's PM practice. You are accountable for engagement-level outcomes, strategic direction, and account growth through trust and delivery excellence.

## Template Usage Notes

- **This is a structural guide, not a content source.** All content comes from the user's skill checklist selections mapped through the inventory categories.
- **Always differentiate L3 vs L4** through scope of impact, stakeholder level, strategic ownership, account growth responsibility, and experience.
- **L4 must signal:** strategic thinking, client advisory, trust-building, account growth, and team support across R&P — not just execution at a higher altitude.
- **Domain section (7) must be customized per engagement** — don't copy-paste from one JD to another.
- **Keep responsibilities action-oriented** — every bullet should start with a verb.
- **Use "Product Manager" as the title**, not "Product Owner." If a client requires PO, use: "Senior Product Manager (Product Owner)."
- **Omit company boilerplate sections** — this template covers PM-specific content only.
- **Do not include skill numbers** in the final JD — these are internal references only.
