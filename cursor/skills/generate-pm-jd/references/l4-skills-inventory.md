# L4 Principal PM — Skills Inventory for Review

> **Purpose:** L4 builds on the confirmed L3 skill set. This document shows (1) which L3 skills elevate in scope/depth at L4, (2) what new skills L4 adds, and (3) what stays the same.
> **Applies to:** Both Traditional PM and AI Builder PM variants (variant-specific skills noted).
> **Reference:** L3 skills inventory (`l3-skills-inventory.md`, Section C — confirmed).
> **Category order:** Matches L3 and consulting industry convention (Deloitte, Accenture, Thoughtworks, Amazon).
> **Skill order within each category:** Sorted by impact on engagement success (highest impact first). New L4 skills placed by impact relative to elevated/unchanged L3 skills.

---

## How to Read This Document

Each section mirrors the L3 inventory structure. For every skill:
- **Same as L3** — no change in expectation
- **Elevated at L4** — same skill, higher scope/depth. L3 description shown for comparison, L4 elevation described.
- **New at L4** — skill that doesn't exist at L3

> Shape product direction beyond a single engagement. Connect product decisions to the client's broader business objectives and long-term value.

| # | Skill | L3 → L4 | L4 Description |
|---|---|---|---|
| D1.1 | Define product vision, strategy, and roadmap aligned to business outcomes | **Elevated** | L3 owns strategy within a single product/engagement. **L4 shapes strategy across the engagement or multiple engagements; connects product decisions to the client's broader business objectives and long-term value.** |
| **D1.2** | **Influence client product strategy beyond the immediate engagement scope** | **New at L4** | Advise client leadership on product direction, portfolio prioritization, and technology bets. | D1.3 | Outcome framing — reframe client feature requests into business outcomes; answer "what result are you trying to achieve?" before committing to a solution direction; ensure the engagement is oriented around outcomes, not outputs | **Elevated** | L3 frames outcomes for their product area within the engagement. **L4 frames outcomes at the program level — reframes entire client conversations from "build me X" to "achieve Y"; coaches the team on outcome thinking and ensures the client's leadership measures success by results, not features shipped.** |
| D1.4 | Prioritize based on business value, user impact, and technical feasibility | **Elevated** | L3 prioritizes within the backlog. **L4 prioritizes across workstreams and makes portfolio-level tradeoff recommendations to client leadership.** |

| D1.6 | Translate business/operational problems into structured product requirements | Same as L3 | Core PM skill at both levels. |
| D1.7 | Balance near-term delivery milestones with long-term scalability | **Elevated** | L3 balances within the engagement. **L4 balances across the engagement lifecycle and factors in post-engagement sustainability for the client.** |

Evaluate competitive approaches and emerging platforms. Inform roadmap with market context. *(Moved from L3 — this is an L4 responsibility in agency context.)* Source: EPAM, Amazon, Slalom |

## D2. Product Discovery & Validation

> Earn the right to build — then define the discovery strategy for the engagement. Frame problems as testable hypotheses, set the research agenda, design experiments that de-risk before committing engineering resources, and ensure discovery compounds into decisions.

| # | Skill | L3 → L4 | L4 Description |
|---|---|---|---|
| D2.1 | Problem framing and hypothesis definition — articulate the customer problem as a testable hypothesis before jumping to solutions; define what success looks like and what would disprove the assumption | **Elevated** | L3 frames hypotheses for their product area. **L4 sets the hypothesis discipline for the engagement — ensures every workstream starts with a testable problem statement, defines what evidence would change direction, and holds the team accountable to discovery rigor before committing to build.** |
| D2.2 | Conduct or coordinate user research (interviews, usability testing, analytics, journey mapping) — understand how users behave, what they need, where they struggle, and what they value | **Elevated** | L3 conducts or coordinates research. **L4 defines the research and insight strategy for the engagement — decides what to research, what methods apply, how findings translate to prioritization, and guides the team on discovery practices.** |
| D2.3 | Data-informed decision making — validate product decisions with qualitative and quantitative evidence; set success thresholds before experiments, not after | **Elevated** | L3 validates individual decisions. **L4 establishes the evidence framework for the engagement — defines what data matters, what thresholds trigger action, how the team builds a shared fact base, and ensures the client adopts evidence-based product thinking.** |
| D2.4 | Experimentation and POC validation — design low-cost experiments (prototypes, POCs, A/B tests, concierge tests) to validate assumptions before committing full engineering resources | **Elevated** | L3 designs and runs experiments for their features. **L4 defines the experimentation strategy for the engagement — decides what deserves a POC vs. going straight to build, sets experiment design standards, and coaches the team on when an experiment has enough signal to act on.** |
| D2.5 | Design collaboration in discovery — partner with design to create concepts, test prototypes with users, and validate UX assumptions before they enter the backlog; own the "what" and "why," design owns the "how it looks and feels" | Same as L3 | Core PM-design partnership at both levels. L4 may shape how the PM-design relationship works across the engagement, but the collaboration itself is the same. |
| D2.6 | Competitive and landscape discovery — understand how the market currently solves this problem, what alternatives users have, and where the opportunity gap exists; feed this into prioritization and positioning | **Elevated** | L3 researches competitors for their product area. **L4 brings outside-in strategic context to the engagement — connects competitive intelligence to client product strategy, identifies market timing risks, and ensures the client isn't building what the market already solved.** |
| D2.7 | Synthesis and insight communication — distill research, experiments, and data into clear insights that drive product decisions; make evidence accessible to the team and stakeholders, not locked in a report | **Elevated** | L3 synthesizes and communicates insights. **L4 sets the insight communication standard for the engagement — defines how discovery findings reach stakeholders, ensures insights connect to strategic decisions (not just backlog items), and coaches the team on turning data into narrative.** |
